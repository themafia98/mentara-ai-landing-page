The phrase "AI interview prep" covers very different products.

Some tools are basically flashcard wrappers. Some generate practice questions. Some offer
resume or behavioral prompts. Some simulate a live interviewer. These are not interchangeable
just because they all use a model somewhere.

## What to evaluate instead of hype

If you are choosing an AI tool for technical interview prep, focus on four questions:

1. Does it make you explain, not just answer?
2. Does it push on weak reasoning with follow-up questions?
3. Does it give you actionable feedback, not generic praise?
4. Can you repeat the loop often enough to build a habit?

That is a much better filter than "has AI" or "supports coding interviews."

## Tool categories that matter

### 1. Question generators

Good for warming up and avoiding blank-page syndrome. Weak for realism, because there is no
real pressure once the question is on screen.

### 2. Coding drill tools

Good for implementation speed, syntax fluency, and pattern reps. Weak for communication,
trade-off defense, and interview pacing.

### 3. AI copilots that explain answers

Good for study support after practice. Weak if they turn the prep loop into passive reading.

### 4. AI mock interview systems

Good when they actually simulate an interviewer: coherent persona, adaptive pressure, live
follow-ups, and a report that reflects what happened in the session.

## Where Mentara fits

Mentara is being built for the fourth category.

The point is not to be a broad "career assistant." The point is to recreate the part of the
loop most candidates under-train: staying sharp while another mind keeps pressing on your
answer.

That means:

- role- and level-aware technical interviews,
- voice or text practice,
- live scoring across multiple dimensions,
- and a report that turns one session into the plan for the next one.

If you are comparing AI prep products, that is the distinction worth making. The most useful
tool is not the one that explains the most. It is the one that changes how you perform when
the room gets uncomfortable.
