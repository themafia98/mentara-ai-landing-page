---

Staff-level interviews are not primarily about whether you can still code.

Of course you need technical depth. But the deeper question is whether you operate at the
level of systems, organizations, and decisions that move more than your own ticket queue.

## What panels tend to probe

At staff level, interviews often look for:

- architectural judgment,
- ability to drive direction without formal authority,
- prioritization across competing constraints,
- technical credibility with broad scope,
- and communication that creates alignment instead of confusion.

That is a very different signal profile from a standard coding loop.

## Why prep often misses the mark

Candidates sometimes keep solving individual problems while undertraining:

- design narrative,
- cross-team influence stories,
- long-horizon trade-offs,
- and how to defend architectural choices under pressure.

That creates a mismatch between title aspiration and interview performance.

## Better questions for your prep

Instead of asking only "could I solve this?", ask:

- Could I frame this problem at the right altitude?
- Could I explain why this trade-off matters now, not just in theory?
- Could I persuade skeptical peers with this answer?
- Would this sound like leverage, or just execution?

Those questions shift the session closer to what staff interviews actually feel like.

## What a useful mock should do

For staff prep, the interviewer should not reward breadth theater. It should push until the
answer becomes concrete:

- what changed because of your decision,
- what constraints drove it,
- what you deliberately did not optimize first,
- and how you handled disagreement.

That is the kind of pressure that makes the gap visible early enough to fix it.
